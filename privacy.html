Privacy Policy

Effective Date: July 9, 2025

Last Updated: July 9, 2025

Baby Bliss by Naava (“we,” “us,” or “our”) respects your privacy and is committed to protecting any personal information you provide to us. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, store, and share information through our website, www.babyblissbynaava.com, and any related services, including sleep training workshops, email communications, and contact forms.

1. Information We Collect

We may collect the following types of information when you interact with our site:

a. Personal Information

You may voluntarily provide us with personal details when:

  • Signing up for a workshop
  • Filling out a contact or intake form
  • Subscribing to our newsletter or email list
  • Sending us a message or inquiry

This may include:

  • Your name
  • Email address
  • Phone number
  • Your baby’s age or sleep concerns (if provided)

b. Non-Personal Information

We may also collect information about your device and visit, such as:

  • IP address
  • Browser type
  • Pages visited
  • Time spent on the site

This helps us improve website performance and user experience.

2. How We Use Your Information

We may use your information to:

  • Respond to your questions or requests
  • Provide information or services you signed up for (like sleep workshop reminders or follow-up emails)
  • Send updates, tips, or promotional content (with your consent)
  • Improve our services and website
  • Analyze website usage and trends

We do not sell or rent your personal information to third parties.

3. Email Communications

If you opt into our email list, we may send occasional updates, sleep tips, and workshop info. You can unsubscribe at any time by clicking the “unsubscribe” link in any email or contacting us directly.

4. Sharing of Information

We only share personal information with trusted third-party service providers who help us operate the website or provide services—such as:

  • Email marketing platforms
  • Website hosting providers
  • Scheduling/payment processors

These providers are required to protect your information and may not use it for other purposes.

We may also disclose your information if required by law or to protect our rights.

5. Cookies and Tracking

Our site may use cookies or similar technologies to track usage and improve performance. You can adjust your browser settings to disable cookies, though this may affect some features of the site.

6. Data Security

We take reasonable steps to protect your personal data using secure technologies and trusted third-party platforms. However, no method of online transmission is 100% secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

7. Children’s Privacy

Our site is intended for adults–specifically, parents and caregivers. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under the age of 13. If you believe a child has provided personal data, please contact us and we will delete it.

8. Your Rights

Depending on your location, you may have rights to:

  • Access the data we hold about you
  • Correct inaccurate information
  • Request deletion of your data
  • Withdraw consent to marketing communications

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at the email below.

9. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Changes will be posted on this page with a new effective date. Continued use of the site after updates means you accept the revised policy.

10. Contact Us

If you have any questions or concerns about this Privacy Policy or how we handle your data, please contact:
